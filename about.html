About Siquijor Fishing Spots and Ports

Siquijor, a serene island in the Visayas region of the Philippines, is home to breathtaking natural beauty and rich marine life. Its tranquil waters are perfect for fishing, attracting both seasoned anglers and curious travelers. Among the island’s jewels are its fishing spots like the Gabayan Bay, Tulapos Marine Sanctuary, and Salagdoong waters, where fishermen enjoy a peaceful and bountiful catch.

Two of the island’s vital entry points — Siquijor Port and Larena Port — serve not just as transportation hubs but also as active fishing areas. Siquijor Port, located in the capital, is ideal for easy access and offers stunning sunset views over calm harbors. Meanwhile, Larena Port, the larger of the two, buzzes with both trade and traditional fishing activities. These ports symbolize the balance between modern logistics and age-old fishing traditions that define the island.

Siquijor's fishing culture is rooted in community, sustainability, and respect for nature. Whether you're casting a line at dawn or watching boats come in at sunset, Siquijor’s fishing experience is more than just a livelihood — it’s a lifestyle, a legacy, and a celebration of island life.
