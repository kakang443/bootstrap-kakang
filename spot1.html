Catamboan Riprap and Mangrooves Spot

Catamboan, located in Siquijor, is a serene coastal area known for its riprap-lined shores and thriving mangrove ecosystems, making it a unique and peaceful fishing spot for locals and visitors alike. The combination of riprap barriers and dense mangrove roots creates an ideal habitat for various marine species, attracting shore anglers who prefer quiet, natural settings. These riprap structures not only protect the coastline from erosion but also serve as shelter for small fish and crabs, while the mangroves function as natural nurseries where juvenile fish like snappers, groupers, and emperors grow. Local fishers often use traditional methods such as bamboo traps and crab nets in the shallows, while some prefer line fishing during early morning or late afternoon, aligning with the tidal shifts that encourage fish activity.
